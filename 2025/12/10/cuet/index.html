Recently, I traveled from Dhaka to Chattogram to participate in Televerse 1.0, the first-ever national Capture The Flag (CTF) competition hosted by CUET. This was my first time visiting the CUET campus, and the experience was significant both as a traveler and a competitor.

The Competition: Televerse 1.0

The event was designed to test technical instincts and problem-solving skills under pressure. The theme, "Step into the shadows of the cyber battlefield," set a serious tone for the challenges ahead. The competition focused on three core pillars: Decrypt, Defend, and Outsmart.

As a national-level CTF, challenges covered a wide range of cybersecurity domains. We spent the day analyzing code, identifying vulnerabilities, and working through complex puzzles to secure flags.

The Team: Blind_Virus

I competed alongside my teammates:

  • Sourav bhai
  • Fahim bhai
  • Momrul bhai

Operating under the name Blind_Virus, we relied on clear communication and collective technical strengths. we face difficult tasks that allowed us to stay competitive throughout the event.

Result and Reflection

After a long day of intense competition, I am proud to share that Blind_Virus emerged as the Champions.

Securing the top spot at the first national CTF at CUET is an honor. The journey from Dhaka was demanding, but the opportunity to test our skills against other talented teams from across the country made it a rewarding experience. I want to thank the organizers at eteteleverse.com for hosting a professional and challenging event.
